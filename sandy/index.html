Identifying Signs of Termite Activity

Knowing what to look for can help you catch a termite problem early. Be on the lookout for:

  • Mud tubes extending from the ground up to your foundation. These serve as protective pathways for subterranean termites.
  • Damaged wood that sounds hollow when tapped. Termites eat wood from the inside out.
  • Discarded wings, often found near windowsills and light fixtures, especially in the springtime when termites swarm.
  • Small holes in drywall or plaster, sometimes accompanied by termite droppings (frass).
  • Swarming termites themselves, particularly during warmer months.

If you notice any of these signs, it's crucial to contact a professional immediately. Early detection significantly reduces the cost and extent of potential damage. Ignoring these signs will only allow the infestation to worsen, leading to more significant and costly repairs down the line. Proactive detection is your best defense against extensive termite damage. We encourage homeowners to conduct regular visual inspections of their property, paying particular attention to vulnerable areas. Even seemingly minor signs can be indicators of a larger, hidden problem beneath the surface. Our team can also provide guidance on what to look for during your own inspections. Recognizing the subtle clues is key to staying ahead of a potential termite problem. Don't hesitate to reach out if you have any suspicions – it's always better to be safe than sorry when it comes to termites.
